The machine guns on the T-34 significantly improved apon the tank's combat effectiveness. Whether using the coaxial machine gun for close-quarters suppression, the hull-mounted MG for self-defense, or the PPSh-41 for personal defense, these weapons provided the T-34 with a multi-role capability that helped it thrive in the diverse and brutal conditions of the Eastern Front during World War II. The inclusion of effective firepower against infantry and light vehicles made the T-34 a highly flexible platform for both offensive and defensive operations.

    DT coaxial machine gun

  • Ammo type: 7.62mm Location: Coaxial, mounted alongside the main gun in the turret. Capability: The DT 7.62mm was the primary coaxial machine gun mounted next to the 76.2mm F-34 main gun in early T-34 models (and later with the 85mm gun in the T-34/85 variant). It was used for engaging infantry, light vehicles, and aircraft while the main gun was aimed at heavier targets like tanks and fortifications. The DT MG was highly effective for close-range combat and suppression fire, providing a complementary armament to the main gun. As a belt-fed, water cooled machine gun, it could fire rapidly at a rate of 900-1,000 rounds per minute. The water cooling system helped prevent overheating during sustained fire, allowing it to engage enemy infantry.

    • PPSh-41 7.62mm Submachine Gun (Personal Weapon)

  • Type: 7.62mm, fully automatic, submachine gun. Location: Stored in the crew compartment (usually in the turret) for close-quarters defense. Capability: The PPSh-41 was a personal weapon provided to the T-34 crew for close-range defense when engaging enemy infantry or in the event of an assault on the tank. Known for its high rate of fire of 900 rounds per minute and its large 71 capacity drum magazine, the PPSh-41 was effective in clearing trenches or defending against infantry assaults near the tank. While it isnt a tank's primary armament, it was a crucial weapon for the crew, especially when they were under direct attack.

    • 7.62mm DT 7.62mm Tank Machine Gun (Hull MG)

  • Type: 7.62mm, belt-fed, air-cooled machine gun. Location: Mounted in the front hull of the T-34, operated by the tank commander or loader. Capability: The DT 7.62mm hull machine gun was a secondary armament used for self-defense and engaging enemy infantry, light vehicles and aircraft while the tank was in motion or stationary. This machine gun had a high rate of fire and was effective for providing suppressive fire when the tank was exposed or during tank movement in open areas. While its primary role was defensive, the hull MG also provided support during ambushes and when engaging enemy infantry forces near to the tank.

    • Ammunition Used by the T-34 MGs

  • 7.62mm Round: The DT and PKT machine guns both used the standard 7.62mm round, which was widely available and produced in large quantities during the war. This ammo type was effective for engaging both soft and armoured targets at close range. High-Explosive (HE) Tracer Ammunition: Used for better target identification and providing an incendiary effect against lightly armoured targets. Armour-Piercing (AP) Ammunition: Occasionally used for engaging unarmoured vehicles and light fortifications.
