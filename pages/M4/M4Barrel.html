The Tiger I, a German heavy tank used during World War II, was equipped with the formidable 88mm KwK 36 L/56 gun. This cannon became one of the most feared weapons on the battlefield due to its exceptional range, accuracy, and armor-piercing capability. The KwK 36 was capable of penetrating the thickest armor of Allied tanks at long distances, particularly effective against Soviet T-34s and British Churchill tanks.

With a barrel length of 56 calibers (4.9 meters), the 88mm gun could fire a variety of ammunition types, including armor-piercing, high-explosive, and incendiary rounds.

The gun's high muzzle velocity, combined with its precision, allowed it to engage targets at ranges of up to 2,000 meters or more.

    PzGr 40 (Armor-Piercing, Fin-Stabilized - APFSDS)

  • Type: A more advanced, fin-stabilized, tungsten core shell. Capability: Significantly better at penetrating thick armor than the PzGr 39. It was used against heavily armored Allied tanks like the Soviet IS-2, though it was less common due to limited production.

    • Gr 39 (High-Explosive - HE)

  • Type: Shell with a high-explosive filling. Capability: Used against soft targets such as infantry, unarmored vehicles, and field fortifications. The HE shell provided significant explosive power but was less effective against armored targets.

    • Sprgr 34 (High-Explosive, Fragmentation - HE-FRAG)

  • Type: Shell designed to create fragmentation upon detonation. Capability: Used primarily for indirect fire, causing widespread damage to enemy infantry, artillery, or unarmored targets. It was effective in soft target engagements but not effective against armored vehicles.

    • Gr 38 (Incendiary - HEI)

  • Type: High-explosive incendiary shell designed to start fires. Capability: Primarily used against fuel tanks, ammunition stores, or unarmored vehicles. It could ignite flammable materials upon impact, causing secondary explosions and fires.

    • PzGr 39 (Armor-Piercing, Capped, Ballistic Cap - APCBC)

  • Type: Armor-piercing shell with a steel core, capped with a layer of copper. Capability: Effective against heavily armored vehicles at long ranges. The ballistic cap improved aerodynamics and increased penetration, especially at higher velocities.
