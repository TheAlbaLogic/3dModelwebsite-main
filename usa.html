Interwar

During this period the americans tested and designed many different types of tanks but all of them were either used and updated later to become offically WW2 Tanks or were never mass produced as they were outdated.

WW2

The M3 Stuart was a light tank used by the US and other Allied forces during World War II. It was also used by several countries in the post-war period.

The M24 Chaffee was a light tank used by the United States and its allies during World War II and the Korean War. It was notable for its speed and mobility.

The M4 Sherman was a widely used American tank in World War II. It was reliable and effective against German tanks but lacked protection and firepower in later stages of the war.

The M26 Pershing was an American heavy tank introduced in the final stages of World War II. It was designed to counter German heavy tanks and saw limited service in the Korean War.

Cold War/ Modern

The M60 Patton was an American main battle tank that served from the 1960s through the 1990s. It featured a 105mm main gun, composite armor, and a powerful engine.

The M1 Abrams is a third-generation American main battle tank. It is highly mobile, heavily armored, and equipped with a powerful 120 mm smoothbore gun. It has served in numerous conflicts since the 1980s.
