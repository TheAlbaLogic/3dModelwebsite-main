Interwar

The Mark 1 tank was the first tank used in combat by the British during World War I. It was heavily armored and armed with machine guns and cannons.

The Mark V tank was a British heavy tank used in World War I. It was armed with six machine guns and had thicker armor than its predecessor, the Mark IV.

The Vickers tank was a British tank used during the interwar period. It was lightly armored and armed with machine guns, and saw action in several conflicts before being retired in 1945.

WW2

The Tetrarch was a British light tank used during World War II. It was small and fast, armed with a machine gun and a 2-pounder gun, and saw action in the North African and Italian campaigns.

The Cruiser Mk IV was a British cruiser tank used during World War II. It was armed with a 2-pounder gun and had a top speed of 32 mph.

The Cromwell was a British cruiser tank used in World War II. It was designed to be fast and had good mobility and firepower, making it effective in battle.

The Comet was a British cruiser tank used during WWII. It was an improved version of the Cromwell, featuring thicker armor and a more powerful engine, but was produced too late to see significant combat.

The Matilda MK II was a British infantry tank used in WWII. It had a thick armor and a powerful gun but was slow and mechanically unreliable.

The Churchill MK VII was a heavy British infantry tank used during WWII. It had thick armor, good firepower, and a slow speed. It played a significant role in the Allied victory in Europe.

Cold War/ Modern

The Conqueror was a British heavy tank developed in the 1950s. It featured thick armor and a powerful gun, but was ultimately replaced by the Centurion as Britain's main battle tank.

The Centurion MK VII was a British post-World War II main battle tank. It featured a powerful 20-pounder gun and advanced technology, making it a formidable force in the battlefield.

The GSR Setter was a prototype British tank developed in the early 1950s. It was a lightly-armored reconnaissance vehicle equipped with a 20mm automatic cannon and a 7.62mm machine gun.

The FV301 was a British light tank designed in the 1950s for reconnaissance missions. It featured a powerful engine, good mobility, and a 76mm gun, but was ultimately never mass-produced.

The Challenger 2 is a British main battle tank that has been in service since 1998. It features advanced armor and weapon systems, and is designed for high mobility and reliability in combat situations.
