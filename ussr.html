Interwar

The Soviet T-26 was a light infantry tank used in the Spanish Civil War, Winter War, and World War II. Over 11,000 were built and it saw action in many conflicts.

The T-35 was a Soviet multi-turreted heavy tank designed and produced in the 1930s. It had five turrets and was one of the heaviest tanks of its time.

WW2

The BT-7 was a Soviet light tank used during World War II, featuring sloped armor and a 45 mm gun. It was known for its speed and agility on the battlefield.

The T-34 was a Soviet medium tank widely used during World War II. It had sloped armor, a powerful 76mm gun, and was highly maneuverable. Over 84,000 were produced.

The KV-1 was a heavy tank used by the Soviet Union during World War II. It was heavily armored and armed with a powerful 76mm gun, making it a formidable opponent.

Cold War/ Modern

The T-54B was a Soviet main battle tank introduced in the mid-1950s. It was an upgraded variant of the T-54, featuring improved armor protection and a new gun stabilizer.

The T-90 is a Russian third-generation main battle tank featuring advanced protection, mobility, and firepower. It has a 125mm smoothbore gun, composite armor, and a low profile turret design.

The T-14 Armata is a modern Russian main battle tank equipped with an unmanned turret, active protection systems, and advanced sensors, making it highly capable on the battlefield.
